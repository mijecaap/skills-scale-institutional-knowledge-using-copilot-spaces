# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile methodology implementation, remove blockers, and foster continuous improvement. They ensure the team adheres to agile practices and maintains a sustainable pace.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, standups, reviews, retrospectives)
- Remove impediments and blockers for the team
- Coach the team on agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from external distractions and interruptions
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and learning
- Track team velocity and help optimize delivery flow

### Goals
- Maintain team productivity and sustainable pace
- Reduce blockers and wait time
- Improve team collaboration and self-organization
- Enable predictable delivery through consistent processes

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups facilitation
- Sprint planning and retrospective sessions
- One-on-ones with team members to address concerns
- Collaboration with Project Manager on risks and dependencies

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Partners to align on schedules, risks, and dependencies; shares responsibility for removing organizational impediments
- **Developers**: Coaches on agile practices, removes blockers, facilitates effective ceremonies
- **Product Manager**: Ensures product backlog is ready and well-groomed for sprint planning

---

## Sponsors

### Role Summary
Sponsors provide strategic direction, financial backing, and executive support for projects. They make high-level decisions and ensure alignment with organizational goals.

### Responsibilities
- Provide funding and resources for the project
- Make strategic decisions on scope, priority, and trade-offs
- Champion the project at the executive level
- Approve major changes to budget, timeline, or scope
- Remove organizational barriers and enable cross-functional collaboration
- Review and approve key milestones and deliverables

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers strategic business value
- Maximize return on investment
- Align project outcomes with organizational priorities
- Enable project success through adequate resources and support

### Typical Communication
- Monthly or milestone-based project reviews
- Executive summaries and business impact reports
- Approval requests for major decisions or changes
- Strategic alignment discussions

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Receives status updates, provides guidance on priorities and resource allocation
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on strategic direction, validates product vision aligns with business goals
- **Stakeholders**: Represents project interests at executive level, facilitates approval processes

---

## External Stakeholders (Key Users)

### Role Summary
External Stakeholders include end users, customer representatives, and other parties outside the core team who have a vested interest in the project outcomes. They provide crucial feedback on usability and validate that deliverables meet real-world needs.

### Responsibilities
- Provide feedback on usability and functionality
- Participate in user acceptance testing (UAT)
- Validate that deliverables meet business needs
- Attend demos and milestone reviews
- Share insights on user workflows and pain points
- Represent the voice of the customer

### Goals
- Ensure solutions are user-friendly and fit for purpose
- Validate that features solve real problems
- Provide early feedback to reduce rework
- Support successful adoption

### Typical Communication
- Sprint demos and feature previews
- UAT feedback sessions
- Surveys and user research interviews
- Retrospective participation for major releases

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Primary contact for gathering requirements and feedback; validates product direction
- **Developers**: Provides real-world context for features, participates in demos
- **QA Specialist**: Collaborates on acceptance testing and validates test scenarios
- **Customer Success**: Partners to ensure smooth adoption and addresses post-launch concerns

---

## QA Specialist (Quality Engineer)

### Role Summary
QA Specialists focus on ensuring software quality through comprehensive testing strategies. They design and execute tests, identify defects, and work with the team to maintain high quality standards throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Design test plans and test cases based on acceptance criteria
- Execute manual and automated tests
- Identify, document, and track defects
- Collaborate on defining Definition of Done and quality gates
- Implement and maintain test automation frameworks
- Perform regression, integration, and performance testing
- Validate fixes and verify acceptance criteria are met

### Goals
- Catch defects early in the development cycle
- Maintain high test coverage and quality standards
- Reduce production incidents through comprehensive testing
- Enable fast, confident releases

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- Test result reports and defect triage meetings
- PR reviews focused on testability
- Quality metrics and test coverage dashboards

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on testability, reviews PRs, pairs on test automation
- **Product Manager**: Validates acceptance criteria, ensures features meet requirements
- **Project Manager**: Reports on quality metrics, highlights quality risks
- **External Stakeholders**: Coordinates UAT activities, gathers feedback on quality

---

## Account Executive / Customer Success

### Role Summary
Account Executives and Customer Success managers focus on post-launch support, customer satisfaction, and ongoing relationship management. They gather usage metrics, collect customer feedback, and ensure customers derive maximum value from the product.

### Responsibilities
- Monitor customer satisfaction and product adoption metrics
- Gather and prioritize customer feedback and feature requests
- Coordinate with support teams on customer issues
- Provide insights for product roadmap based on customer needs
- Facilitate customer training and onboarding
- Track and report on customer success metrics
- Ensure smooth handoff from delivery to operations

### Goals
- Maximize customer retention and satisfaction
- Drive product adoption and value realization
- Identify upsell and expansion opportunities
- Reduce churn through proactive engagement

### Typical Communication
- Post-launch reviews and customer check-ins
- Monthly business reviews with key accounts
- Feedback summaries for product and engineering teams
- Retrospective participation to share customer insights

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Provides customer feedback and market insights; collaborates on roadmap priorities
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on release timelines, participates in retrospectives
- **Support Team**: Escalates critical issues, shares patterns in customer problems
- **External Stakeholders**: Direct customer interface, gathers requirements and validates solutions
